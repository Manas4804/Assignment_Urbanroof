# Detailed Diagnostic Report (DDR)

## 1. Property Issue Summary
The property, Flat No. 103, exhibits widespread dampness and leakage issues affecting multiple internal areas including the Hall, Common Bedroom, Master Bedroom, Kitchen, and Common Bathroom, as well as the Parking Area ceiling. Key observations include skirting level dampness, efflorescence on walls, tile hollowness, open tile joints, and general plumbing issues. External wall cracks and a duct issue are also noted as potential sources of water ingress.

## 3. Probable Root Cause
The probable root causes for the observed issues include: leakage from concealed plumbing, damage to the Nahani trap and/or brickbat coba under tile flooring in bathrooms, gaps and blackish dirt in tile joints, loose or rusted plumbing joints (e.g., flush tank, shower, angle cock, bibcock, washbasin), internal WC/Bath/Balcony leakage, cracks in the external wall, issues with the external ducting, and outlet leakage from the bathrooms of the flat above (Flat No. 203).

## 4. Severity Assessment: **Critical**
**Reasoning:** The property exhibits widespread and persistent dampness and leakage affecting multiple critical areas including living spaces, bathrooms, and the structural parking area below. The leakage is described as 'All time' and attributed to significant issues like concealed plumbing failures, damaged waterproofing (Nahani trap/brickbat coba), and external structural defects (cracks, duct issues). The presence of efflorescence indicates long-term moisture exposure, which can lead to structural degradation and health hazards if not addressed promptly and comprehensively.

## 5. Recommended Actions
- Conduct a thorough investigation to pinpoint the exact locations of concealed plumbing leaks and repair them.
- Address and repair any damage to Nahani traps and brickbat coba under tile flooring in all affected bathrooms.
- Remove existing damaged grout and re-grout or replace compromised tile joints in the Common and Master Bedroom Bathrooms.
- Inspect and rectify all loose or rusted plumbing joints associated with fixtures like flush tanks, showers, angle cocks, bibcocks, and washbasins.
- Repair all observed cracks on the external wall and resolve the identified duct issue to prevent further water ingress from the exterior.
- Collaborate with the residents of Flat No. 203 to investigate and repair the source of outlet leakage from their Common and Master Bedroom Bathrooms affecting Flat No. 103's ceiling.
- Implement appropriate waterproofing measures for the parking area ceiling and the Common Bathroom ceiling after the source of leakage is resolved.
- Allow all damp-affected internal walls to dry completely, then undertake necessary plaster repairs, apply a suitable waterproofing primer, and repaint.

## 6. Additional Notes
The property is 11 years old, and no previous structural audit or repair work has been reported, which may have contributed to the accumulation of these issues over time. The inspection scored 85.71% with 'WC' and 'External wall' flagged as areas of concern.
